# Factor & Function Reference

This reference lists all the top level categories and subcategories that factors and functions are grouped into. Search in here for the appropriate category and then read the specific category markdown file. Each category file contains all subcategories and associated factors.

## Ratios & Statistics ([ratios-and-statistics.md](ratios-and-statistics.md))

Calculated information that combines more than one fundamental data point.


### Valuation

Comparisons between fundamental data points and price.

### Valuation Projected

Other Valuation ratios that involve analyst estimates

### Yield

Factors related to dividend and shareholder yields

### Margins

Functions that return total revenues divided by a data point (usually an income statement line).

### Profitability

Return on Equity, Return on Assets and Return on Investments.

### Per Share Ratios

Fundamental line items divided by the number of fully diluted shares.

### Efficiency

Turnover and per-employee ratios.

### Financial Strength

Quality tests, particularly relating to debt or dividend payout.

### Other

Other ratios and statistics.

### Advanced

Beneish M-Score, Piotroski F-Score, Alman Z-score.
## Financials ([financials.md](financials.md))

Financial statement line items from filings (annual, quarterly or semiannual) and press releases. Figures are standardized by the data vendor.


### Income Statement

Lines from the (consolidated) statement of income.

### Balance Sheet

Lines from the (consolidated) balance sheet.

#### Assets-Current

Owned items that are identified by the company as being collectible or liquid enough to be disposed of within the next 12 months.

#### Assets-Noncurrent

Owned items that are identified by the company as being illiquid.

#### Liabilities-Current

Debts that are identified by the company as due within the next 12 months.

#### Liabilities-Noncurrent

Debts that are identified by the company as due more than 12 months in the future.

#### Shareholders Equity

The difference between total assets and total liabilities. The lines herein indicate how much more a company is worth than the the debt that it has incurred.

#### Shares

Basic, fully diluted and most recent share counts.

### Cash Flow Statement

Lines from the (consolidated) statement of cash flows.

#### Operating

Cash flows generated by operations, including inventory changes.

#### Investing

Cash flows generated by investing activities, including capital expenditures.

#### Financing

Cash flows generated by financing activities, including debt service and dividend payments.

#### Summary

Cash flow lines that do not fit into the three general activities, such as net flow of cash.
## Fundamentals ([fundamentals.md](fundamentals.md))

Other company fundamental data that is not a direct financial statement line item from filings or press releases.


### Actuals

Actuals are companies' historical financial data, such as earnings, sales, and net income. Unlike fundamentals that come from official filings submitted to government agencies, Actuals are collected primarily from company press releases or from brokers.

### Company

Qualitative and informational data about the company. Includes country of domicile and number of employees.

### Corporate Actions

Flags and data relating to the timing and history of corporate events, including splits and M&A.

### Dividends

Dividend-related data, including yield and IAD.

### Filings Related

Information about the earnings reports filed by the company to regulatory agencies.

### Listing Related

Factors and functions related with the traded listing or stock

### Insider & Institutional

Data relating to institutional & insider ownership of a company's shares.

### Short Interest

Data relating to the short interest of a company's shares.
## Estimates ([estimates.md](estimates.md))

Data relating to consensus analyst estimates.


### Estimate Functions

Consensus estimate functions

### EPS Estimates

Earnings per share consensus estimates with statistics, number of analysts, and previous values for trend analysts.

### Sales Estimates

Sales consensus estimates with statistics, number of analysts, and previous values for trend analysts.

### Other Estimates

Other consensus mean estimates

### EPS Revisions

Factors to indicate number of analysts revisions EPS consensus estimate Up or Down. NOTE: Data is only updated in the weekend

### Surprises

Analysts surprises

### Historical

Analysts last estimates for previous periods and surprises compared to the Actuals

### Recs & Opinions

Analysts average recommendation and long-term growth estimate.
## Technical ([technical.md](technical.md))

Functions to calculate and create information and triggers based on price or volume.


### Performance

Price changes and total return.

### Price

Functions related to price EOD time series

### Volume

Functions related to volume EOD time series

### Moving Average

Functions to calculate moving averages of time series.

### Accumulation

Functions to calculate money flows in stock trading, with a particular eye toward volume analysis.

### Trending

Functions to analyze trend strength and direction in the market of a stock.

### Pattern

Gap Up, Gap Down.

### Oscillators

Functions that either return a positive or a negative condition at any given time.

### Volatility

Quantifications of the variability of a time series.
## Advanced Functions ([advanced-functions.md](advanced-functions.md))

Advanced functions that provide things like summary statistics, ranks, and other specific analytics.


### AI Factor

Functions that return AI model predictions

### Ranking

Functions that return the rank from a ranking system.

### FHist Functions

Functions that calculate Point In Time values (PIT) by changing the observation date. An example use case for these functions is to find stocks that are cheap relative to their previous valuations.

### Loop Functions

Functions that operate on historical values using a loop formula which must contain the CTR iterator. Example use cases for these functions include counting how many quarters have positive cashflow, and finding companies that have increased their dividend every year.

### Loop Regression

Functions that calculate regressions on historical values using a loop formula which must contain the CTR iterator. Use cases for these functions include finding stocks with upward, well defined trends for sales.

### Relative vs. History

Functions that compare and normalize values longitudinally against historical values for the same stock.

### Relative vs. Group

Functions that compare and normalize values cross-sectionally vs. other stocks in a group. Use cases for these functions include screening for stocks that are in the top 10% for a metric in their sector.

### Group Summary Statistics

Functions that calculate cross-sectional summary statistics for a group. An example use case for these functions is to find companies that are cheap relative to their industry or sub-sector.

### Screener Only

Misc functions that only work in the screener.

### Watchlists, Holdings, & Opinions

Functions that access holdings in watchlists, portfolios, or accounts as well as functions that access stock opinions.

## Misc ([misc.md](misc.md))

Functions that do not fit into any other category.


### Time Series IDs

Series IDs that can be used as the series parameters in functions, or in the charts section. For example you can retrieve the latest value of the SP500 Index with:<br><br>Close(0,$SP500)<br><br>See the Full Description of each ID for more examples.

### Macro Series IDs (Adjusted)

FRED macro series that are seasonally adjusted.

### Macro Series IDs (Unadjusted)

FRED macro series that are not seasonally adjusted.

### Group

Functions that perform calculations on groups of stocks or that identify individual stocks.

### Math

Mathematical functions, including logs, absolute values and random numbers.

### Regression

Regression functions and statistics

### Regression Stats

Statistics for a previously computed regression.

### Set

Functions that perform mathematical functions on finite data sets.

### Utility

Functions that perform logical evaluations, provide timing information or setting variables.

### Constants

Identification codes of data points and series accessible across many functions.

### Operators

Logical and mathematical operators in common use across the site.
